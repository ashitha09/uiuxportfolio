VitalCare: Designing a Patient-Centered Mobile Medical Appr

Project Overview

VitalCare is a patient-focused mobile app that makes booking care, viewing medical records, tracking vitals, and communicating with clinicians simple and reassuring. The app emphasizes clarity, speed, and trust — delivering essential health tasks with a calm, accessible UI so users of all ages can manage appointments, prescriptions, and test results without confusion.

The vision was to craft a lifestyle-enhancing platform that merges aesthetic simplicity with robust functionality. Through a calming interface and personalized dashboards, users can manage their home environment intuitively and securely.

Timeframe

4 Weeks

My Role

UX Research, UI Design, Prototyping, Information Architecture, Visual Design, Usability Testing

Tools

Figma, Notion, Zoom, Illustrator

The Challenge

  • Make booking and clinician access frictionless
  • Present lab results and prescriptions clearly (no jargon)
  • Smooth login & signup flow
  • Provide a simple vitals-tracking dashboard
  • Ensure privacy and trust signals are visible at key touchpoints

Objectives

  • Create a calm, legible mobile interface for patients aged 18–65+
  • DReduce appointment booking time to less than 3 taps from home screen
  • Surface critical items (upcoming appointment, unread test results, medication reminders) on the home dashboard
  • Design interactions that are accessible (large tap targets, readable typography, clear error states)

Wireframes

Lo-fi sketches prioritized clarity in navigation for appointments, prescriptions, and search. In the hi-fi stage, we applied a calm color palette, medical iconography, and accessible layouts to ensure trust and ease of use for patients across age groups.

Lo-Fi Wireframe of VitalCare booking flow

Branding & Visual Design

  • Soft blues + muted greens (trust + calm) with high-contrast call-to-action accents for CTAs.
  • Typography: Large, humanist sans for body copy (readability), slightly heavier weight for key numbers (BP, glucose).
  • Iconography: Simple line icons for actions (book, results, chat).
  • Imagery / Tone: Warm, friendly photography for empty states and onboarding; neutral medical illustrations for educational content.

High-Fidelity Screens

  • Home: Hero banner, Lookbook, Gift Guide, Lifestyle shots
  • Discover: Brand story, materials section
  • Products: Grid with filters and sorting
  • Contact: Form, social links, optional map

Usability Testing

Methodology: Remote usability testing with 6 participants (ages 21–65, including 2 with chronic conditions) using a Figma prototype and Zoom observation. Tasks focused on frequent patient activities such as booking care, tracking vitals, and reviewing reminders.

Test Scenarios

  • Complete sign-up and log in
  • Book an appointment with a clinician from the home dashboard
  • Review upcoming appointments and reminders
  • Send a secure message to a doctor

Key Findings

  • Task Completion: All participants successfully completed the core tasks.
  • Appointment Booking: Average booking flow took fewer than 3 taps from the home screen.
  • Dashboard: Participants valued seeing critical items (next appointment + reminders) immediately.
  • Vitals Tracking: Card-based entry was intuitive; several users asked for long-term visualization of progress.
  • Accessibility: Older participants requested larger tap targets and clearer error states during sign-up.

User Feedback

“Booking was so much faster than my current hospital app.”
“I liked how I could see my upcoming appointment as soon as I opened the app.”
“The vitals tracker was simple — I’d love to see trends over weeks.”

Iterations

  • Increased calendar tap size and contrast for better accessibility.
  • Added short contextual tooltips on lab results explaining clinical ranges.
  • Reordered Home quick-actions so “Book” and “Messages” are first for faster access.
